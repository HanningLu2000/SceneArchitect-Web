Abstract

With the widespread adoption of VR devices and immersive content, the demand for progressive 3D scene generation is rapidly increasing. An effective virtual scene should satisfy 1) structural consistency for free-viewpoint exploration and 2) scene-level semantic coherence with the input description. However, when a scene is expanded progressively from limited observations, existing pipelines are prone to error accumulation: small depth or alignment errors can gradually amplify into geometric distortion, while locally synthesized views may drift from the intended scene semantics, resulting in inconsistent layouts and illogical content. To address these limitations, we propose SceneArchitect, a progressive 3D scene generation framework built on two core components: Structural Consolidation, which leverages a foreground-centric reconstruction strategy together with cross-view depth alignment and fusion to preserve geometric coherence during scene growth, and Semantic Grounding, which maintains a global reference view for completion-region localization and scene-level context preservation, followed by context-aware prompt refinement to keep local synthesis controllable without drifting from the overall semantics. To further stabilize the final 3D representation, we introduce a depth-regularized Gaussian refinement strategy with staged optimization, improving robustness under imperfect monocular depth priors while retaining fine-grained appearance. Extensive experiments on diverse indoor and outdoor scenes show that SceneArchitect mitigates geometric and semantic error accumulation, achieving better structural coherence, semantic consistency, and overall visual quality than representative baselines for progressive 3D scene generation.
